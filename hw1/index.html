Overview

What is a Network? A network is a group of interconnected devices, referred to as nodes, that are able to communicate with one another. A network is made up of:

  • Structure - the physical or wireless links that connect these devices and enables them to communicate
  • Infrastructure - The hardware, softwarem and communication protocols that support network communication. Examples include computers, routers, servers, and protocols such as HTTP, TCP, and UDP.
  • Purpose - What the network is intended to communicate. A general example would be to consider the postal service whose purpose is to deliver packages or mail from one destination to the other.

While there are many kinds of networks around the world, such as advised for us to maintain throughout our occupational persuits, computer networks supports modern technologies such as smartphones, servers, vehicles, as well as smart devices and support functions like printing documents off a printer.

What is the Internet? The internet refers to the global network of computer networks that use the standardized communication protocols mentioned above. This means that all the individual networks scattered across the globe are all connected to form a world-wide network known as the internet. These individual networks could range from home and school networks to mobile service provider networks such as AT&T. Large enterprise networks are also part of the internet and are operated by companies including Amazon and Google.
